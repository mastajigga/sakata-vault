# Light Article: Iluo, the Secret of the Doubles

*Nkundi ya bakoko: "Moko azali eloko te, mibale bazali eloko."*
— Proverb of our ancestors: One is nothing, two are something.

Listen to the whisper of the forest, my child. The *Iluo* (the twins) are much more than two babies born on the same day. In our Sakata culture, they are the guardians of the shared breath and messengers of the water spirits.

### 1. A Blessing from the River
It is said that the Iluo come from the Kisakata River. They are not an anomaly, but a grace of fertility sent by the ancestors. Their birth requires special rites: the father builds a double hut with a single hearth, symbolizing two bodies for a single soul.

### 2. The Intelligence of the Double
Inspired by the wisdom of the Bazia tribe and the great Chief Nienie Augustin, we know that twins carry complementary strength. Often, one is the protector (the warrior) and the other is the sage (the visionary). Together, they form a balance that nothing can break.

### 3. Names of Strength
To protect them, we give them mirrored names: *Kofi* and *Kofi*, or *Nimi* and *Nimi*. This is not out of a lack of originality, but to bind their destinies so closely that even the whirlpools of life cannot separate them.

### 4. The Link to the Invisible
Iluo speak to the fish and sense the coming rain. They are our bridges to the invisible. Today, whether they are doctors or village chiefs, they still carry that "double sight" that helps our people walk straight in the modern world.

Remember this, young sprout: to respect an Iluo is to respect the very balance of creation.
